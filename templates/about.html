What is this?

The ORACLE (On-Line Physician Decision Support Tool for the Management of Oral Anticoagulation in Patients with Atrial Fibrillation Undergoing Percutaneous Coronary Intervention) Project is an alliance of several academic physicians motivated by the gap in knowledge and unmet needs in the management of patients with Atrial Fibrillation that undergo PCI. The team is composed by General Cardiologists, Interventional Cardiologists, Arrhythmia and Hematology Specialists, and knowledge translation experts. The purpose of this group is to provide and facilitate the use of a decision tool for the management of oral anticoagulation in patients with atrial fibrillation undergoing PCI with the ultimate goals of improving the appropriate use of guidelines and improving outcomes and net clinical benefit.

Concerned with the lack of evidence and conflicting recommendations regarding the role of dual antiplatelet and triple therapy management in patients with atrial fibrillation undergoing PCI the ORACLE investigators have conducted preliminary surveys that indicate that the majority (80%) of physicians surveyed use the CCS guidelines, however only 33% correctly selected the appropriate recommendations demonstrating a significant gap in knowledge.
